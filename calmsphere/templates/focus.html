Focus Meditation

10 minutes  ·  Requires a candle or small object

The mind wanders — that is what it does. Focus meditation trains you to notice the wandering and gently return, again and again, to a single point of attention. Each return is a rep of mental strength.

1 Set Up Your Focal Point

Place a candle flame, a small dot on the wall, or any still object at eye level about an arm's length away. Sit comfortably with your spine straight. Take three deep breaths to arrive.

2 Soft Gaze

Let your eyes rest softly on the focal point — not staring intensely, but holding a relaxed awareness. Notice its color, shape, and stillness. When thoughts arise, simply label them "thinking" and come back to the point.

3 Return & Repeat

Your mind will wander many times — this is not failure. Each time you notice, silently say "return" and bring your attention back to the focal point. Continue for 10 minutes, gently and patiently.

The point is not to never wander. The point is to always return.
