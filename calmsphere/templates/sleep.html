Sleep Meditation

20 minutes  ·  Best done in bed

Sleep cannot be forced — it can only be invited. This practice gently guides your body and mind into deep rest, releasing the day's tension so that sleep may find you naturally.

1 Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Get comfortable in bed. Starting with your feet, tense the muscles as tight as you can for 5 seconds, then release completely. Move up to calves, thighs, hips, belly, hands, arms, shoulders, jaw, and face. Feel the wave of relaxation wash over each part.

2 Deep Breathing

Breathe deeply into your belly — inhale for 4 counts, hold gently for 4 counts, exhale slowly for 6 counts. Let each exhale be a little longer, a little softer, as if you are sighing yourself toward sleep.

3 Peaceful Visualization

Imagine a place where you feel completely safe and at peace — a quiet beach, a forest glade, or a cozy room. Notice the colors, sounds, and sensations. Let this scene fill your mind. If thoughts drift in, gently return to your peaceful place and drift deeper.

You don't have to fall asleep. You just have to rest. Sleep will follow.
